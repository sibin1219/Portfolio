Ratan Tata


Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was a chairman of the Tata Group from 1990 to 2012, and interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017. He continues to head its charitable trusts.[2][3] In 2008, he received the Padma Vibhushan, the second highest civilian honour in India, after receiving the Padma Bhushan, the third highest civilian honour in 2000.He is the son of Naval Tata, who was adopted by Ratanji Tata, son of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. He graduated from the Cornell University College of Architecture with a bachelor's degree in architecture.[5] He joined Tata in 1961, where he worked on the shop floor of Tata Steel. He later succeeded J. R. D. Tata as chairman of Tata Sons upon the latter's retirement in 1991. Under his tenure the Tata Group acquired Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus, in an attempt to turn Tata from a largely India-centric group into a global business. Tata is also one of the largest philanthropists in the world, having donated around 60–65% of his income to charity.


Life Journey


Ratan Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is an iconic Indian industrialist and philanthropist. As the scion of the esteemed Tata family, he steered the Tata Group to new heights during his illustrious career. Ratan Tata's journey in the business world began in the 1960s when he joined the Tata Group. His leadership skills and visionary approach became evident as he took charge of the conglomerate. Under his guidance, the Tata Group expanded globally, making strategic acquisitions, including the notable purchase of Jaguar Land Rover. Beyond his role in business, Ratan Tata is renowned for his commitment to social causes. His philanthropic initiatives, especially in the fields of education and healthcare, have left a lasting impact on society. Ratan Tata served as the chairman of Tata Sons until his retirement in 2012, leaving a legacy of ethical leadership and corporate responsibility. Despite stepping down from his formal role, he remains an influential figure, continuing to inspire the business community and the younger generation. Some Notable Companies and Initiatives Associated With Ratan Tata

Tata Consultancy Services(TCS)

TCS was established before Ratan Tata's chairmanship, but it grew substantially under his leadership to become one of the largest IT services companies globally.

Tata Motors

Ratan Tata led the acquisition of Tata Motors and played a pivotal role in the expansion of the company's presence in the automotive industry, both in India and internationally.

Tetley(Tata Tea)

The acquisition of Tetley by Tata Tea under Ratan Tata's leadership marked the group's entry into the global beverage market.


Corus Group

Another significant acquisition was the British steelmaker Corus Group by Tata Steel, positioning the Tata Group as a major player in the global steel industry.
